\int\frac{x^2-1}{x^2+1}dx
\int\frac{x^2+1}{x^2+1}dx-\int\frac{2dx}{x^2+1}\\
\implies \int dx-2\int\frac{dx}{x^2+1}\\

x=\tan\theta\\
\implies 1+x^2=1+\tan^2\theta = \sec^2\theta\\
\implies dx=\sec^2\theta d\theta

x-2\int\frac{\sec^2\theta d\theta}{\sec^2\theta}\\
\implies x-2\int d\theta\\\\
\implies x-2\theta+A\\
\implies x-2\tan^{-1}x+A
